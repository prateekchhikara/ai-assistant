Diljit Dosanjh, born on January 6, 1984, is a prominent Indian singer, actor, film producer, and television personality. Originating from Dosanjh Kalan, Jalandhar, he embarked on his career in 2002, gaining recognition in Punjabi music through albums like "Smile" (2005) and "Chocolate" (2008), and collaborating with Yo Yo Honey Singh on "The Next Level" (2009). His entry into acting began with a cameo in the Punjabi film "Mel Karade Rabba" (2010), followed by a leading role in "The Lion of Punjab" (2011). In Bollywood, Dosanjh made a notable debut in 2016 with the crime thriller "Udta Punjab," earning the Filmfare Award for Best Male Debut and a nomination for Best Supporting Actor. His versatility extended to films like "Good Newwz" (2019), earning him another Best Supporting Actor nomination. Dosanjh's musical prowess is evident with chart-topping albums such as "G.O.A.T." (2020) and "MoonChild Era," both listed on the Billboard Canadian Albums Chart. In 2023, he made history as the first Indian artist to perform at the prestigious Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival. Alongside his career achievements, Dosanjh enjoys swimming and dancing, showcasing his multifaceted talent and enduring popularity across music and cinema.
